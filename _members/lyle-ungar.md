Lyle Ungar is a Professor of Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania, with additional faculty appointments in Psychology, Bioengineering, Genomics and Computational Biology, and Operations, Information and Decisions. He has published over 300 articles and supervised more than two dozen PhD students. His research develops natural language processing and explainable AI methods for psychological and medical research — including analyzing social media and cell phone sensor data to understand the drivers of physical and mental well-being. He co-directs the World Well-Being Project and is a co-founder of Positive Acuity.
