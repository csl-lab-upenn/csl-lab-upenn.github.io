Agrima Seth is a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Michigan School of Information, advised by Kentaro Toyama. Her research sits at the intersection of HCI and NLP, examining cultural biases in language models and how cultural values shape user interactions with technology. She has held research internships at Microsoft Research, Snap Inc., and CERN, and was awarded the 2024 Google PhD Fellowship. She is currently on the faculty and industry job market, seeking roles in human-centered and cross-cultural AI research.
